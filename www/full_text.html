OFFERING PROCESS

Method for completing the transaction:

The method for completing the offering involves the following steps: (1) an investor registered with the funding portal identifies a desired offering and communicates an investment commitment to purchase securities in the offering by completing a subscription agreement, (2) the funding portal confirms investor eligibility and then has the subscription agreement countersigned by the issuer, (3) the investor transfers the funds required to purchase the offered securities to the funding portal’s designated escrow agent, (4) once the target offering amount is achieved or exceeded through investor commitments collectively, the issuer would be able to close the transaction on the original target date or an earlier date with at least five business days prior notice to the investors, (5) the funds would be released by the escrow agent to the issuer and the issuer would issue the securities to the investor. Note, there is also a method for communicating material changes to an offering which requires the issuer to notify the investors of such material change so that each investor has the ability to reconfirm their investment commitment prior to completing the transaction.

Method for delivering the securities to investors:

The issuer will record all investments in book entry form so that no certificates would be delivered. The fully executed Subscription Agreement would serve as a written record of the transaction and would be available on the funding portal via the investor dashboard. The investor would also receive email confirmation of the successful closing of the offering which would confirm the number of securities purchased and the amount paid.

Method for investor cancellation of a commitment:

Method for investor cancellation of a commitment:Investors may cancel an investment commitment at any time up until 48 hours prior to the applicable deadline. The process for any such cancellation is for the investor to log onto the funding portal and indicate a cancellation by selecting such option in connection with the applicable offering as listed on the investor’s dashboard. With respect to specific scenarios: (1) if the target offering amount is not met prior to the target closing date, then the funding portal will automatically cancel the investment commitment and notify the investor of the return of funds, (2) if the issuer reaches the target offering amount prior to the deadline identified in the offering materials, then it may close the offering early (if it provides notice about the new offering deadline at least five business days prior to such new offering deadline) and the investor would be able to cancel the investment commitment before the 48-hour period prior to the new offering deadline, (3) if the issuer notifies the investor of a material change to an offering, then the investor must reconfirm his or her investment commitment after the material change is made to the offering, or the investor’s investment commitment will be cancelled and the committed funds will be returned.

RISKS TO PURCHASERS

Risks to purchasers related to minority ownership:

Purchasers of minority ownership have risks relating to limited control, limited information and limited rights. Minority owners have no control over the marketability and value of their purchased securities and they may be required to hold onto their minority ownership position for an indefinite period.

  • Minority owners have no right to participate in any management decisions of the company or the day-to-day operations. There is the risk that the majority owners make decisions or take actions that a minority owner thinks is bad for the issuer. A minority owner has limited ability to influence the governance and operations of an issuer.
  • Minority owners have limited rights to information beyond those filings that are required by law to be made available. There is the risk that minority owners may be denied immediate information about an issuer’s performance or corporate affairs.
  • Minority owners have limited rights, if any, to demand current distributions from an operating business. Majority owners likely have the right and power to avoid making any distributions of profits at their discretion. There is also the risk that the management of the issuer pursues a spending plan that causes the issuer not to generate any profits to be distributed
  • Minority owners have limited rights, if any, to have their interest bought out. There is the risk that a minority owner will want to sell their interest and they will have no right to force the company to buy them out. There will also be liquidity risk if there is no secondary market for the issuer’s securities.
  • Minority owners have limited input into fundamental corporate changes such as sale of the issuer. There is a risk that a sale could be structured in a way to avoid certain payouts to minority owners, such as a sale of assets over time with the proceeds reinvested in another business.
  • Minority owners may have interests that are different than shareholder-officers or shareholders that are part of the management team of an issuer. There is the risk that minority owners perceive a conflict of interest if, for example, the managers that are shareholders receive a significant raise. Additionally, there is the risk that a minority owner will want to remove a manager or board member but have no independent power to do so.

Risks to purchasers associated with corporate actions:

The issuer may take certain corporate actions that could impact investors that purchase the offered security.

  • Regarding the additional issuances of securities, there is the risk that an investor has their ownership percentage diluted if, for example, the issuer decides to raise additional amounts in the future. There is also the risk that an investor is excluded from the opportunity to participate in future fund-raising so that they are unable to maintain their ownership percentage.
  • Regarding the authorization of new securities with different rights, there is the risk that a future investor is able to obtain rights that are superior to the rights afforded to the purchasers of the security available in this offering.
  • VARIABLE: Regarding the repurchase of securities by the issuer, there is the risk that investors feel pressured to sell their securities to the issuer without fully participating in or capturing the potential increase in value of such securities.
  • Regarding a sale of the issuer or of assets of the issuer, there is the risk that any such transaction fails to maximize the value of the issuer’s securities. The investor will be required to rely on the business judgement of the management team and the Board of Directors of the issuer so there can be no guarantee that the investor will receive the full market value of their securities.
  • Regarding transactions with related parties, there is the risk that the issuer is placed in a position that creates the possibility of a conflict of interest. The issuer may choose to participate in transactions with affiliates and related persons, with limited disclosure required to investors.
